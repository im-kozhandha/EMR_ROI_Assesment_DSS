# Insights from the Project

## 1. ROI increases with digitization

As more parts of the hospital become digital, the ROI improves.

Even small levels of digitization can start giving positive returns.

---

## 2. Error reduction gives the biggest benefit

Most of the savings come from reducing errors, not from saving time or paper.

This was one of the most important observations.

---

## 3. High-error hospitals benefit more

If a hospital already has a lot of errors, switching to EMR gives much higher returns.

More inefficiency means more room for improvement , therefore high ROI and Payback.

---

## 4. Payback depends on savings

If monthly savings are high, the hospital recovers its investment faster.

But if setup cost is high, it may take longer.

---

## 5. Digitization does not need to be 100%

Hospitals don’t need to fully digitize everything at once.

Even partial adoption can give good results.

---

## 6. Time savings depend on workflow

If EMR takes the same time as paper, then there is no time benefit.

So implementation matters as much as the system itself.

---

## 7. Break-even is very important

Break-even tells the minimum digitization needed to avoid loss.

This helps in planning how much to digitize initially.

---

## Final takeaway

EMR is not just a technical upgrade — it’s a business decision.

The biggest value comes from reducing inefficiencies, especially errors.

Even small steps towards digitization can create meaningful impact.
